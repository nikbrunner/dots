Never propose the following for Black Atom Industries logo work:

- Dot-with-orbital-rings atom marks (electrons-around-nucleus iconography)
- Fake heritage subtext like "EST. 2024", "EST. YYYY", or tagline filler under the wordmark
- Gradients, glows, bevels, skeuomorphism, rounded corners
- Planetary / solar-system metaphors beyond the existing eclipse/crescent interpretation

**Why:** Nik called orbital-ring atom marks "forbidden and cliché" during the Claude Design logo prompt session (2026-04-18). The brand is "black atom" in the vault/archive sense, not the physics-textbook sense — orbital rings pull it toward science-fair aesthetic and away from the 1970s NASA / DHARMA / datasheet north star in `docs/design-language.md`.

**How to apply:** When drafting logo prompts, reference images, or mark proposals for BAI, explicitly exclude these directions. Permitted atomic references are the filled black dot itself, the wordmark `•` substitution for "o", and eclipse/sphere treatments.
